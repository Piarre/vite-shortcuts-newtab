# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: January 20, 2026**

## Overview

Chrome Shortcuts Tab ("the Extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle your information when you use our browser extension.

## Data Collection

**We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.**

The Extension operates entirely on your local device. All data, including:
- Website shortcuts
- Categories
- Color preferences
- Any other settings

are stored locally in your browser using the Chrome Storage API and never leave your device.

## Data Storage

All user data is stored locally on your device using:
- Chrome's local storage mechanism
- No external servers
- No cloud synchronization (unless you enable Chrome Sync)

## Third-Party Services

The Extension does not use any third-party analytics, tracking, or advertising services.

When you perform a web search using the search bar, you are redirected to Google Search. This interaction is subject to [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

## Permissions

The Extension requests the following permissions:
- **Storage**: To save your shortcuts and preferences locally on your device

These permissions are used solely for the Extension's core functionality and not for data collection.

## Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or trade any user data with third parties because we do not collect any data.

## Changes to Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected in the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy.

## Contact

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please contact us through the Chrome Web Store listing or create an issue on our GitHub repository.

## Your Rights

Since all data is stored locally on your device:
- You have full control over your data
- You can delete all data by uninstalling the Extension
- You can manually clear data through your browser settings

## Compliance

This Extension complies with:
- Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
